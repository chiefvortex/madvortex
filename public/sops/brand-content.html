The North Star

Before anything ships, it passes one test: does it match the vibe? Correct grade. Tight cuts. Purpose-driven B-roll. No dead air.

The Seven Non-Negotiables

Rule Standard If It Fails
Hook First First 1.5 sec: bold statement, visual pattern interrupt, or open loop. No friction = failed hook. Reshoot it.
Clean Grade Warm, slightly desaturated. Film-adjacent, not influencer-adjacent. Natural skin tones. No heavy filters. Re-grade.
Tight Cuts Zero dead air. Every frame earns its place. Pause > 0.8 sec: cut it or cover with B-roll. Retrim.
B-Roll With Purpose Every cutaway illustrates the point, establishes context, or creates visual rhythm. Remove or replace.
Audio Is King Crisp voice. No room echo. Background music at 15–20% of voice level. Re-record or re-mix.
Captions Mandatory 85% of short-form consumed on mute. Burned-in, bold, high-contrast, word-by-word. Never a wall of text. Fix before review.
Vertical-First 9:16. Always. Horizontal is a secondary derivative. Re-export.

Footage Ingestion

Two phases. Phase 1 is one-time. Phase 2 runs every week. Both are mandatory.

Phase 1: Backlog Sprint

There are 2–3 TB of footage scattered across Google Drive, Google Photos, and offline disks. This gets sorted before the weekly pipeline runs.

1
Dump
Create VORTEX_CLIP_BANK/_UNSORTED. Copy everything from all sources into it. Do not delete originals until sorting is complete.
2
First Pass — Bulk Sort
Watch each clip at 2x. Assign to a Level 1 category. Move to the correct folder. Rename per the convention. Unusable clips go to _UNSORTED/_REJECTED.
3
Second Pass — Subcategory
Within each Level 1 folder, sort into Level 2 buckets. Every clip must have a home.
4
Sync
Sync the entire Clip Bank to the offline backup disk. Verify no file is missing.

Phase 2: Ongoing Weekly

Day Action Owner Deliverable
Sunday Transfer the week's footage to WEEKLY_INTAKE on Google Drive Naveen Raw footage in Drive
Monday Sort, rename, and file every clip. Complete by EOD. Devansh All clips filed
Tuesday Flag clips strong enough to anchor a standalone video. Devansh Hero clips flagged
Friday Sync Google Drive to offline backup. Devansh Backup current

Clip Extraction Rules

Format: Export in the rawest usable format. iPhone 17 Pro Max footage stays in ProRes or HEVC at native 4K. Compression happens only at final export.
One Idea Per Clip: If Naveen covers three topics in one take, split into three clips.
Handles: 2-second handles at head and tail of every clip.
Audio Sync: If external audio was used, sync before filing. Unsynced footage never enters the Clip Bank.

The Pipeline

Seven stages. No stage gets skipped. Each has an owner, a deliverable, and a gate.

Figure 2 — 7-Stage Video Pipeline (All stages mandatory, no skips)

Stage 1: Ideation

Every Monday, Naveen logs 3–5 topic ideas. Each entry must contain all five elements.

Element Detail
Hook Written out in full. The first thing the viewer sees or hears.
Core Message One sentence. The single takeaway.
B-Roll Needs What supporting footage you need. Pull from Clip Bank or shoot new.
CTA What the viewer does after watching (follow, comment, share, click link).
Platform Instagram, LinkedIn, X, or all three. Format constraints per platform.
Gate: If Naveen cannot say "I would stop scrolling" to the hook, it goes back to draft. Devansh has authority to reject weak hooks.

Stage 2: Scripting

Section Duration Purpose What Happens
Hook 0–1.5 sec Pattern interrupt Bold claim or unexpected visual
Context 1.5–8 sec Set up the problem Why this matters
Meat 8–40 sec Deliver the value The insight, lesson, or take
Payoff 40–50 sec Land the point Emotional close or reframe
CTA 50–60 sec Direct the viewer Follow, comment, share

Duration target: 45–90 seconds for Reels/Shorts. Up to 3 minutes for LinkedIn. Owner: Naveen (with Claude AI assist). Time Budget: 20 minutes per script, maximum.

Stage 3: Shoot

Lens Clean: Wipe the iPhone lens. Number one cause of soft footage.
4K 30fps Minimum: ProRes if storage allows.
Vertical Framing: Eyes in the upper third. Room for captions at the bottom.
Stable Mount: Tripod or gorilla pod. Handheld only for organic B-roll.
Audio Check: 5 seconds of room tone before rolling. Lavalier preferred. iPhone mic only if within 2 feet.
Lighting: Face a window. No overhead fluorescents. Golden hour or open shade outdoors.
Multiple Takes: Minimum 3 per talking head. Energy peaks on take 2 or 3.
B-Roll Buffer: After every talking head session, shoot 5–10 minutes of contextual B-roll at the same location.

Owner: Naveen. Camera: iPhone 17 Pro Max (primary). Deliverable: Raw footage uploaded to WEEKLY_INTAKE.

Stage 4: Edit

1
Assembly
Pull the strongest take from the Clip Bank. Lay talking head on timeline per the script.
2
Trim
Cut all dead air, filler words, false starts, repeated points. Be aggressive.
3
B-Roll
Search the Clip Bank by folder and filename. Layer B-roll at natural transitions and wherever visual interest drops.
4
Color Grade
Apply the master LUT. Adjust exposure and white balance per clip. Every clip in one video must match.
5
Captions
Burned-in, brand font, verbatim. Lower third placement with screen-edge margin.
6
Music & SFX
Background music from approved library at 15–20% of voice level. Transition SFX only where they serve pacing.
7
Brand Elements
Lower third on first appearance, logo watermark, outro CTA. No intro animation on Reels/Shorts.
8
Platform Versions
Separate exports per platform with correct specs.

Owner: Editor (Devansh). Tools: Premiere Pro or DaVinci Resolve. Deliverable: V1 rough cut in the REVIEW folder. Time Budget: 4–6 hours per video.

Stage 5: Internal Review

Naveen reviews within 24 hours. Feedback is timestamped, specific, and actionable. Not "make it better." State what's wrong, exactly where, and what the fix is.

The 24-Hour Rule: If Naveen does not send feedback within 24 hours, V1 auto-approves and moves to final export. The pipeline does not wait for the CEO. Period.

Reviewer: Naveen. Presenter: Devansh. Max Revision Rounds: 2. Upload Format: REVIEW_[TOPIC]_V1_[DATE].mp4

Stage 6 & 7: Export & Publish

Platform Ratio Resolution Max Length Format
Instagram Reels 9:16 1080×1920 90 sec H.264 MP4
LinkedIn Video 9:16 or 1:1 1080×1920 3 min H.264 MP4
Twitter/X 9:16 or 16:9 1080×1920 2:20 min H.264 MP4

Export naming: FINAL_[PLATFORM]_[TOPIC]_[DATE].mp4

Publish: Naveen publishes from personal accounts. Caption, hashtags, posting — that's Naveen's. Brand voice stays with the founder. Editor does not have posting access.
Archive: Editor moves final files to 06_FINALS/FN_PUBLISHED, renamed with the published date.
Performance Log: 48 hours after publishing, Naveen screenshots metrics (views, likes, shares, saves, comments) and logs them in the Content Calendar. This is how next week's ideation gets smarter.

Quality Gates

Seven checkpoints. A video does not advance until it clears its gate.

Gate Criteria Who Checks If It Fails
G1: Hook Would you stop scrolling? First 1.5 sec tested. Devansh + Naveen Reshoot or rewrite
G2: Audio Voice clear, no echo, music balanced. Devansh Re-record or re-mix
G3: Color All clips match, skin tones natural. Devansh Re-grade
G4: Captions 100% accurate, no typos, brand font. Devansh Fix before review
G5: Brand All brand elements present, correctly placed. Devansh Add missing elements
G6: Pacing No dead frames, no filler, tight throughout. Naveen (review) Retrim
G7: CTA Clear call-to-action in final 5 seconds. Devansh Add CTA card
No gate skips. Ever. If a video fails a gate, the specific issue is fixed before moving to the next stage. No exceptions.

Rules of Engagement

These override everything. Vortex has already paid the price for not having them.

Rule 1
No Scope Creep — Script defines video. New ideas mid-edit = next week's video.
Rule 2
Max 2 Revision Rounds — V1 → V2 → V3. If broken after 2, script was wrong.
Rule 3
24h Review SLA — Naveen reviews within 24h or V1 auto-approves.
Rule 4
Publish or Kill — 5 business days in REVIEW = escalation. No response after 24h = archive.
Rule 5
Actionable Feedback Only — What is wrong. Where (timestamp). What the fix is.
Rule 6
Clip Bank is Sacred — Never delete. Never rename outside convention. Discipline is power.
Rule 7
One Video = One Idea — Can't summarize in one sentence? It's two videos. Split it.
These are not preferences. These are the rules. Follow them exactly.
