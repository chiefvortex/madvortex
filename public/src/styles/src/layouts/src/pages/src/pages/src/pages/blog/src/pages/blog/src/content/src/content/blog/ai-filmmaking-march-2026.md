The month that changed the AI production landscape. 12+ major model releases in a single week. Here's what actually matters for filmmaking.

## Video Generation — Multi-shot consistency is solved

- **Runway Gen-4**: Best quality + character persistence across scenes. Hollywood post-production teams are already integrating it.
- **Kling 3.0**: Native 4K at 60fps. 40% cheaper per second than Runway. 3–15 second sequences with subject consistency across different camera angles.
- **Google Veo 3.1**: Deep integration with Drive and YouTube Studio. The ecosystem play.
- **Seedance 2.0**: Highest Elo score in blind tests. China-only until Q2 — worth watching, not usable yet.
- OpenAI killed the standalone Sora app. Video generation is now baked into ChatGPT subscriptions only.

## Frontier Models — The gap compressed to weeks

- **GPT-5.4**: 1.05 million token context window. Three variants (Standard, Thinking, Pro). Scored 83% on GDPVal — human expert level.
- **Gemini 3.1 Ultra**: Native multimodal reasoning across video, audio, and text simultaneously.
- **NVIDIA Nemotron 3 Super**: 120B total parameters, 12B active per forward pass. Built for multi-agent workflows.
- **Mistral Small 4**: Topped open-source reasoning benchmarks on March 3.
- **MiniMax M2.5**: Chinese model rivaling Claude Opus at a fraction of the cost.
- The Model Context Protocol crossed **97 million installs** — it's foundational infrastructure now, not experimental.

## Image Generation — Five tools, five lanes

- **Midjourney V8**: Completely rewritten engine. 5x faster, native 2K output. Still the aesthetic benchmark.
- **Flux 2** (Black Forest Labs): Photorealism king. Skin textures that fool people in blind tests.
- **Ideogram v3**: The only tool that reliably renders text inside images.
- **GPT Image 1.5**: Replaced the DALL-E branding. Solid all-rounder.
- **Adobe Firefly 4**: IP indemnification included. The safe commercial choice.

## Production Workflow — AI is already in Hollywood post

- **AI dubbing** hit quality parity: 50–70% cost reduction, 20+ languages viable for indie productions.
- **Real-time rendering**: Decart's Lucy 2 enables live video-to-video editing. Directors interact with scenes, not prompts.
- Hollywood editors are already integrating AI for scene stabilization, color correction, object removal, and edit suggestions based on emotional content analysis.

## The Bottom Line

The pitch isn't "AI is cool" anymore. It's "production infrastructure using tools that just hit maturity this month." Multi-shot consistency + AI dubbing + real-time rendering = a real service offering, not a demo reel.
